The three main types of research questions are descriptive, comparative, and causal. Descriptive questions describe a phenomenon, comparative questions compare two or more groups, and causal questions determine the cause-and-effect relationships.

A good dissertation question needs to be clear, realistic, focused, and researchable. It should address a gap in the existing literature and be specific enough to guide your research.

In order to write a dissertation question, you need to identify a broad topic of interest, plus review existing literature, identify gaps or unresolved issues, and formulate a specific, researchable question that addresses one of these gaps.
